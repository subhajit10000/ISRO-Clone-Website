A clone website inspired by ISRO, the Indian Space Research Organisation, replicates the structure, tone, and functionality of the official space agency portal while serving as a learning project, template, or informational hub. The homepage typically opens with a full width banner showcasing a launch vehicle like PSLV or GSLV lifting off, accompanied by a concise tagline about advancing space technology for national development. The navigation bar is clean and minimal, with sections such as Home, About, Missions, Launchers, Satellites, Applications, Careers, and Contact. 

The About section summarizes the organization’s history, vision, and major milestones, mirroring ISRO’s journey from sounding rockets to lunar and Mars missions. A Missions page lists key programs like Chandrayaan, Mangalyaan, Aditya-L1, and Gaganyaan, each with a short overview, launch date, objectives, and current status. The Launchers section details vehicle families, payload capacity, and recent flight records using tables and simple diagrams. Similarly, the Satellites page categorizes communication, earth observation, navigation, and scientific spacecraft with icons and brief specs. 

To reflect ISRO’s public outreach, the clone includes an Applications area explaining how space tech supports weather forecasting, disaster management, agriculture, and navigation through services like NavIC. A media gallery presents high resolution images and video thumbnails of launches, satellite deployments, and mission control. For engagement, there is a news or updates feed with cards for press releases, countdown timers for upcoming launches, and links to live webcasts. 

The Careers tab outlines scientist, engineer, and technician roles, eligibility, and a mock application form. The website design uses a deep blue and white palette, ISRO style typography, and responsive grids to work across devices. Interactive elements such as hover effects on mission cards, accordions for FAQs, and a contact form with map integration add realism. While not affiliated with ISRO, the clone demonstrates web design skills, content organization, and appreciation for India’s space program, making it useful for students, developers, or educators creating space themed projects.
